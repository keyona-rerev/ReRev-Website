Where AI Actually Helps

The interactive tool embedded in this guide shows all 14 stages of the journalism workflow. Five of these stages are where AI can significantly enhance your process. Here's how to think about each one:

1. The Fuzzy Idea Stage

You know that jumbled mess of thoughts you have when a story idea first hits? Instead of letting it rattle around in your head for days, talk it out with an AI using voice-to-text. Dump everything. Then ask it to help you identify gaps, organize your thinking, or spot what you're missing.

This isn't AI doing the thinking for you. It's AI helping you think more clearly.

2. Research and Angle Discovery

Once you've chosen an angle, ask: "Based on this angle, what gaps exist in my research? What perspectives am I missing?" Use it as a thinking partner to identify blind spots before you've invested hours going down the wrong path.

3. Interviewing

Convert your interview recordings to searchable text. This isn't revolutionary, but it's resource primingâ€”making your reference materials easier to navigate so you can spend more time analyzing and less time hunting for that one quote.

4. The Outline Check

Before you draft, pause. Feed your outline to an AI and ask: "Based on this, what will readers assume my bias is? Whose story is getting told, and whose isn't?" This forces you to examine your subjective choices before they're baked into the full draft.

5. Claim and Assumption Analysis

After drafting, ask AI to identify: What claims are you making? What assumptions underlie them? What perspective is centered? This helps you see the editorial choices you madeâ€”some conscious, some not.

What AI Can't Do (And Why That Makes You Better)

AI cannot give you objectivity, because objectivity doesn't exist. We are all subjective beings processing reality through lived experience. The angle you choose, the stakeholders you weight, the story you tellâ€”these are editorial choices that shape whose narrative gets heard.

Here's the twist: AI can help you see your subjective lens more clearly. It cannot remove that lens. Nor should it.

This is where the integrity enhancement happens. When you ask AI to identify whose story is being told in your outline, or what assumptions underlie your draft's claims, you're developing a working self-awareness of biases that would otherwise remain hidden. Natural Language Is the New Keyword Search

We grew up learning advanced keyword search as a skill. You knew how to craft the perfect Google query, how to click through multiple links, how to connect dots across sources.

LLMs change this. For about 80% of inquiries, you can now just ask conversationally: "How does a city budget work in context?" No keyword gymnastics required. This is a massive reduction in cognitive loadâ€”which frees up mental energy for the work that actually requires your expertise.

Three Mishaps to Watch For

AI Off the Rails

If AI keeps giving you incorrect information, that's a signal. You need better primary dataâ€”interviews, verified sources, original documents. AI can only work with what it knows, and sometimes what it knows is wrong.

AI Purgatory

Spending more time iterating with AI than it would take to do it yourself. When this happens, step back. Either refine your approach or just do the task manually. You're in the driver's seat.

Treating AI as Truth

AI is not a journalist. It's a tool. Your discernment, verification, and ethical judgment are irreplaceable. Use AI to work smarter, not to outsource your thinking.
