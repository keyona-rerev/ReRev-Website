Building Persuasive Materials with AI

The Philosophy

Your success completely lies in the agency of others. That’s not me being dark, it’s just reality. You have an idea, but what you don’t have is allocated resources and buy-in.

At ReRev Labs, we believe sales and marketing are not job roles. They’re skills every working professional needs. The new age of AI has only made that more clear.

A big part of selling is succinctly, quickly, and effectively communicating your ideas. The difference between “that sounds interesting” and “let’s move forward” often comes down to someone’s ability to understand what you’re trying to communicate. Not just understand it, but visualize it in a way that makes it easy to act on.

When you ask someone to evaluate an idea verbally or through text, they have to hold it all in their head. They have to imagine it. They have to translate your words into meaning.

When you show someone a visual, a clean professional one-pager or interactive pitch, it becomes real. That idea goes from intangible to concrete. It’s something they can show their colleagues. It’s something they can act on.

The Skill: Using AI to Generate HTML and CSS

Traditionally, creating polished custom sales materials meant either hiring a designer or spending hours in design tools like Canva. But now, with AI and some basic HTML and CSS knowledge, you can create professional branded one-pagers in minutes.

This is a workflow I do sometimes a dozen times a week. Whether I’m creating quick reference materials for a client or full-scale solutions, this is a staple of how I communicate and get people to buy into what I’m pitching.

Step 1: Establish Your Copy

Start with the most important part: the content. The entire purpose of using HTML and CSS is not just to make it cute, but to make it real. But you can’t bring to life something you haven’t ironed out first.

Have an iterative conversation with your LLM of choice (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or others). Work through questions like: What problem does this solve? Who’s the ideal person to hear this? What action do I want them to take? What do they care about? What’s the end benefit? What resources are needed to pull this off well?

Pro-tip: Use SpeechNotes (or your preferred voice-to-text tool) to freely converse your thoughts instead of typing. Not only is this faster, it’s move effective.

Step 2: Generate the HTML and CSS

Once your copy is locked in, ask the AI to turn it into a finished product using HTML and CSS (occasionally JavaScript). You’re creating a stylized, cohesive, put-together piece either as a one-pager or simple website.

When it comes to format, I usually choose between an SVG, a one-pager, or a single-scroll website.

Pro tip: LLMs are really good at mimicking Apple’s branding. If you want a more refined look, that’s your go-to for it to emulate.

You don’t need to be overly descriptive about what you want it to look like on the first pass. A brief description is enough. It’s much easier to iterate on a draft than to try to invent everything upfront. This is about reducing cognitive burden for yourself, too.

Step 3: Deploy and Customize

I usually paste the code into CodePen (free, browser-based, no setup required). Tweak colors, fonts, and spacing to match your brand. You now have a live shareable link you can send to anyone.

If you want it as a PDF or image instead of a link, use free HTML-to-PDF converters or HTML-to-JPEG tools. To turn the the code you made into a HTML file, simply open the “Notebook” app on your laptop, paste the code into a new file, save as *name of your choice+ “.html”*, close out the Notebook app. Upload the HTML file to the converter.

Why This Matters

Instead of a long email or multiple back-and-forth conversations, they can immediately start visualizing and get behind it. Instead of a generic PowerPoint, you’re sending a custom interactive page that feels premium.

This lesson came together in sharp focus at a VC training where my business partner and I had to create six presentations in three days. We won all but the first one—and only because we didn’t yet understand the power of this process.

Here’s what we did differently: we spent our time as a team on the most important part, the content. We recorded our conversations and had an AI pull out the core insights. Then I worked with an AI to produce copy that took those unique, experience-backed insights—the ones that could only come from human interaction and years of experience.

We didn’t rely on the LLM to create its own information. We gave it a strong foundation. It had a basic understanding, but it couldn’t have gotten specific without our human expertise layered in. That’s what it looks like to take this higher.

Then we followed the exact process laid out above: AI turned it into a single-scroll HTML/CSS website with a navigation bar at the top. It felt crisp and clean. Our competitors used AI presentation makers and came out with presentations that looked cute but weren’t functional. We ended up winning the presentation side challenges and the overall competition.

The Outcome

By the end of this process, you’ve built at least one custom sales/persuasive artifact using AI and HTML/CSS without having to learn how to code. You’ve employed coding literacy to create something with tangible value for your career tomorrow.

If you’re currently employed: Is there an idea you’re trying to get more people behind? With less than 30 minutes, you can create this artifact and get momentum behind it.

If you’re pitching yourself or a prospective client: Instead of sending an email with your hand out, send something visual and interactive. Create movement and then create more movement.
