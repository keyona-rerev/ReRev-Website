Building Persuasive One-Pagers with AI

The Philosophy

Your success completely lies in the agency of others. That's not me being dark, it's just reality. You have an idea, but what you don't have is allocated resources and buy-in.

At ReRev Labs, we believe sales and marketing are not job roles. They're skills every working professional needs. The new age of AI has only made that more clear.

A big part of selling is succinctly, quickly, and effectively communicating your ideas. The difference between "that sounds interesting" and "let's move forward" often comes down to someone's ability to understand what you're trying to communicate. Not just understand it, but visualize it in a way that makes it easy to act on.

Key Insight: When you show someone a visual, a clean professional one-pager or interactive pitch, it becomes real. That idea goes from intangible to concrete.

The Skill: Using AI to Generate HTML and CSS

Traditionally, creating polished custom sales materials meant either hiring a designer or spending hours in design tools like Canva. But now, with AI and some basic HTML and CSS knowledge, you can create professional branded one-pagers in minutes.

This is a workflow I do sometimes a dozen times a week. Whether I'm creating quick reference materials for a client or full-scale solutions, this is a staple of how I communicate and get people to buy into what I'm pitching.

Step 1: Establish Your Copy

Start with the most important part: the content. The entire purpose of using HTML and CSS is not just to make it cute, but to make it real. But you can't bring to life something you haven't ironed out first.

Have an iterative conversation with your LLM of choice (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or others). Work through questions like:

  • What problem does this solve?
  • Who's the ideal person to hear this?
  • What action do I want them to take?
  • What do they care about?
  • What's the end benefit?
  • What resources are needed to pull this off well?

Pro-tip: Use SpeechNotes (or your preferred voice-to-text tool) to freely converse your thoughts instead of typing. Not only is this faster, it's more effective.

Iterate until the copy is tight and persuasive. Put yourself in their shoes.

Step 2: Generate the HTML and CSS

Once your copy is locked in, ask the AI to turn it into a finished product using HTML and CSS (occasionally JavaScript, but start with HTML and CSS). You're creating a stylized, cohesive, put-together piece either as a one-pager or simple website.

When it comes to format, I usually choose between an SVG, a one-pager, or a single-scroll website.

Pro tip: LLMs are really good at mimicking Apple's branding. If you want a more refined look, that's your go-to for it to emulate.

You don't need to be overly descriptive about what you want it to look like on the first pass. A brief description is enough. It's much easier to iterate on a draft than to try to invent everything upfront. This is about reducing cognitive burden for yourself, too.

Step 3: Deploy and Customize

I usually paste the code into CodePen (free, browser-based, no setup required). You can log in with Google and be active within 60 seconds.

Tweak colors, fonts, and spacing to match your brand. You now have a live shareable link you can send to anyone.

If you want it as a PDF or image instead of a link, use free HTML-to-PDF converters or HTML-to-JPEG tools. Both are browser-based. Note: Sometimes you can copy and paste into the converter, other times you'll have to upload an HTML file. To turn the code you made into an HTML file:

  1. Open the "Notebook" app on your laptop
  2. Paste the code into a new file
  3. Save as *name of your choice* + ".html"
  4. Take note of where the file is being saved
  5. Close out the Notebook app
  6. Upload the HTML file to the converter

Why This One-Pager Approach Matters

Instead of a long email or multiple back-and-forth conversations where you're trying to describe what you want, they can immediately start visualizing and get behind it.

Instead of a generic PowerPoint, you're sending a custom interactive page that feels premium or a polished one-pager in less than 15 minutes.

This lesson came together in sharp focus at a VC training where my business partner and I had to create six presentations in three days on different topics: LP fundraising strategy, exit processes, leading founders through exits, etc. We won all but the first one - and only because we didn't yet understand the power of this process.

Real-World Win: We spent time on content, used AI for formatting, and won against competitors using AI presentation makers. Theirs looked cute; ours was functional and persuasive.

The Outcome: From Idea to Action

By the end of this process, you've built at least one custom sales/persuasive artifact using AI and HTML/CSS without having to learn how to code. You've employed coding literacy to create something with tangible value for your career tomorrow.

Your One-Pager Action Plan:

If you're currently employed: Is there an idea you're trying to get more people behind? With less than 30 minutes, you can create this one-pager and get momentum behind it.

If you're pitching yourself or a prospective client: Instead of sending an email with your hand out, send something visual and interactive. You can get this workflow down to a science and be spitting these out in less than 10 or 15 minutes and get momentum behind it. Because there's nothing more important than momentum in business. Create movement and then create more movement.
